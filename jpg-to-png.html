When working with digital images, one of the most common tasks is converting between file formats. JPG and PNG are two of the most widely used formats: JPG is great for smaller file sizes, while PNG supports transparency and better quality for graphics. Many users worry—will the quality drop when converting from JPG to PNG? The good news: you can convert JPG to PNG without losing quality when you use a lossless export.

Why convert JPG to PNG?

  • Transparency: PNG supports transparent backgrounds—perfect for logos and UI assets.
  • Lossless: PNG uses lossless compression, preserving crisp edges and fine details.
  • Editing: PNG keeps quality stable across multiple edits and saves.

Tips for best quality

  • Start from the highest quality source you have.
  • Scaling down reduces pixels permanently—decide based on usage.
  • PNG won’t remove existing JPG artifacts; it simply avoids adding new ones.
